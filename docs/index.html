Running in 3 steps

One-time setup, ~10 minutes. The wizard picks an AI model that fits your hardware.

1

Install Ollama — the free local AI engine Kaiwa uses by default.

2

Download Kaiwa above, unzip, and run:

macOS:    ./setup.sh  then  ./run.sh
Windows:  .\setup.ps1  then  .\run.ps1
3

Open http://localhost:8130 — the onboarding wizard takes it from there. こんにちは！

Questions, answered

The things everyone asks before trying it

Is it really free?
Yes. Kaiwa is open source under AGPL-3.0 — no account, no subscription, no trial, no telemetry. If you choose to connect a cloud AI (Gemini/OpenAI/Claude), you use your own API key and pay that provider directly — Gemini has a free tier. The default local AI costs nothing.
Do I need to be technical to install it?
A little, for now: download the zip, unzip it, and run two scripts — the setup wizard handles all the AI setup after that. One-click installers (.dmg / .exe) that start everything automatically are on the roadmap.
What kind of computer do I need?
Realistically ~8 GB of RAM. No GPU needed — a CPU-only machine runs a small model at conversational (not instant) speed. The first-run wizard checks your hardware and recommends a model that will actually feel good on it. Kaiwa is developed on an aging Intel MacBook — if it runs there, it runs on your machine.
Does it work offline?
Yes. After the one-time setup downloads (AI model, dictionary, speech model), everything — chat, voice, corrections, dictionary, reviews — works with the Wi-Fi off.
Where does my data go?
Nowhere. Everything lives in one SQLite file on your disk (data/kaiwa.db). You can export it, back it up automatically, move it between Mac and Windows, or delete it. Messages only leave your machine if you deliberately connect a cloud AI with your own key.
I'm a total beginner — is it for me?
Yes — pick N5 and Kaiwa speaks simply, praises what you get right, explains mistakes in English, and gives you three suggested replies whenever you're stuck. Furigana, romaji, and translations can be toggled per conversation, and 10 guided lessons walk you from greetings to keigo.
How do I get the really nice voices?
Out of the box Kaiwa uses your OS's built-in Japanese voice. For much nicer ones, download the free VOICEVOX engine and unzip it into Kaiwa's vendor/ folder — it's picked up automatically and a voice picker (with previews) appears in Settings.
Can I use it on my phone?
Yes. Your computer runs Kaiwa; your phone connects to it securely from anywhere with the free Tailscale app — Settings shows a QR code to scan, then "Add to Home Screen" makes it feel like a native app, mic and all.

About Kaiwa

Why this exists

Kaiwa is built by @kroogies — a Japanese learner who wanted real conversation practice without a subscription, and without sending every clumsy sentence to someone else's server.

It started as a personal tool on an old Intel MacBook and grew into a full tutor: conversations, corrections, a dictionary, reviews — all running locally, because language practice is personal.

It's open source under AGPL-3.0, which keeps it free forever — for everyone, from anyone. Next up: real one-click installers, nicer onboarding, and a stable Windows release.
